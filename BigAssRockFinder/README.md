## Big Ass Rock Finder (B.A.R.F.)

<p align = "center">
  <img src = "B.A.R.F Logo.png" width = "500">
</p>

The B.A.R.F. is a scintillation detector that utilizes a Hamamatsu R1513 photomultiplier tube, five-inch organic scintillator, and zinc-sulfide screen. It is specifically designed to quickly detect radioactive mineral specimens in large low-background environments. 

The housing consists of four components. The bottom can holds the organic scintillator, the zinc-sulfide screen, and the light-blocking aluminized polymer film. The top housing holds the PMT and voltage divider and the top cap holds the female BNC connector. Between the bottom can and the top housing, a gasket is placed such that half of the gasket is inside a recessed ring of each component. This gasket ensures the detector is completely light tight at the window-scintillator interface.
